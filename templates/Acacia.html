Acacia Tortilis

السمر

Basic Information

Acacia Tortilis, also known as the Umbrella Thorn Acacia, is a hardy tree native to the Middle East and Africa. It's well adapted to dry, desert conditions and is highly valued for its shade and the resilience it offers in arid landscapes.

  • Type: Tree
  • Lifespan: 30–50 years
  • Height: Can reach up to 15 meters
  • Blooming Season: Spring

Care & Benefits

Acacia Tortilis is easy to grow in sandy, well-drained soil and is tolerant of drought. It is highly beneficial for its shade and is often used in desert landscaping.

  • Care Level: Easy
  • Benefits: Provides shade, drought-tolerant, wildlife-friendly
  • Caution: Can have thorns that may cause injury

Growing Conditions

Sunlight: Full Sun

Climate: Hot and Dry

Temperature Tolerance: Up to 45°C, Frost-sensitive

Soil: Sandy, Well-drained

Placement: Outdoor Only

Watering: Low (Very drought-tolerant once established)

Medicinal & Toxic Properties

The pods and leaves of Acacia Tortilis have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. However, certain parts of the tree may be toxic to livestock, so caution should be exercised in grazing areas.

  • Uses: Traditional use for pain relief and inflammation
  • Toxicity: May be toxic to livestock if consumed
