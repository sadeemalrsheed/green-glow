Jojoba

الجوجوبا

Basic Information

Jojoba is a hardy desert shrub native to arid regions. Its seeds produce a unique oil used widely in cosmetics and skincare. It is increasingly cultivated in Saudi Arabia for both ecological and commercial value.

  • Type: Shrub
  • Lifespan: Up to 100 years
  • Height: 1–3 meters
  • Blooming Season: Spring

Care & Benefits

Jojoba is low-maintenance and highly drought-resistant. Its oil is valued in skincare for being similar to human sebum, making it a natural moisturizer.

  • Care Level: Easy
  • Benefits: Produces valuable oil, low water usage, soil stabilization
  • Commercial Use: Cosmetics, moisturizers, conditioners

Growing Conditions

Sunlight: Full Sun

Climate: Arid and Semi-Arid

Temperature Tolerance: Up to 50°C, cold-sensitive below 0°C

Soil: Sandy, Well-drained

Placement: Outdoor

Watering: Very low once established

Economic & Environmental Role

Jojoba helps reduce desertification by stabilizing sandy soils. Its oil is a renewable resource and has growing commercial demand globally in the cosmetic and pharmaceutical industries.

  • Environmental Role: Drought-resistant and erosion control
  • Oil Use: Eco-friendly alternative to animal-based and synthetic oils
