Bougainvillea

الجهنمية

Basic Information

Bougainvillea is a fast-growing, thorny vine known for its bright and colorful bracts that resemble flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is widely cultivated in Saudi Arabia for fences, walls, and pergolas.

  • Type: Climbing Vine/Shrub
  • Lifespan: Over 20 years with care
  • Height: Can grow up to 10 meters
  • Blooming Season: Mostly Spring to Fall (year-round in warm areas)

Care & Benefits

Bougainvillea is low-maintenance but thrives best with pruning and plenty of sunlight. It adds stunning color to gardens and can be shaped into hedges, arches, or wall covers.

  • Care Level: Easy to Moderate
  • Benefits: Visual appeal, drought-tolerant, suitable for landscaping
  • Note: Has thorns; handle with care

Growing Conditions

Sunlight: Full Sun

Climate: Hot and Dry

Temperature Tolerance: Up to 42°C

Soil: Well-drained, slightly acidic

Placement: Outdoor preferred (can grow in pots)

Watering: Low once established (allow soil to dry)

Fun & Cultural Facts

Bougainvillea is often mistaken for having colorful flowers, but the true flowers are small and white, surrounded by bright bracts. It’s named after the French explorer Louis Antoine de Bougainville.

  • Symbolism: Passion, liveliness, and tropical beauty
  • Common Uses: Garden fences, walls, pergolas, urban landscaping
